## The Prompt

```
BEFORE YOU START: Quick readiness check
- Can you clearly answer: (1) Why this donor? (2) Why this amount? (3) Why now?
- If not, pause and gather more information before running this prompt.

INFORMATION GATHERING CHECKLIST (mark what you have)

REQUIRED:
- [ ] [DONOR NAME] and [DONOR INTERESTS/PRIORITIES] (2–4 themes, e.g., first-gen students, climate resilience)
- [ ] [PROJECT NAME] and [TOTAL PROJECT COST]
- [ ] [PRIMARY ASK AMOUNT] and [YEARS] (pledge period)
- [ ] [ORGANIZATION NAME] and [MISSION/FOCUS]
- [ ] [SIGNER NAME, TITLE] and [CONTACT INFO] (email and phone)

RECOMMENDED:
- [ ] [CURRENT FUNDS RAISED] and [FUNDING GAP]
- [ ] [ALT ASK 1] and [ALT ASK 2] (typically ~60% and ~150% of primary ask)
- [ ] Donor touchpoints in past 12–18 months (gifts, visits, meetings, event attendance, emails)
- [ ] [REPORTING CADENCE] (quarterly, biannually, annually)
- [ ] [RECOGNITION OPTIONS] and [PRIVACY PREFERENCE]
- [ ] [GEOGRAPHY/COMMUNITY] where the project operates

OPTIONAL (enhances personalization):
- [ ] [OPTIONAL: ANECDOTE/STORY] (2–3 sentences linking donor to mission)
- [ ] [MATCH/CHALLENGE INFO] (source, timing, ratio, cap)
- [ ] [MEETING DATE/DEADLINE]
- [ ] [ED/BOARD APPROVAL STATUS] (e.g., ED approved; Board Dev. Committee endorsed)
- [ ] [PER-UNIT COST EXAMPLES] (2–3 concrete items)
- [ ] Key risks and mitigations
- [ ] [FORMAT PREFERENCE: formal letter / PDF proposal / email proposal]
- [ ] [TONE: FORMAL / WARM / CASUAL]

PROMPT:

Draft a personalized major gift proposal for [DONOR NAME] in support of [PROJECT NAME] at [ORGANIZATION NAME], tailored to [DONOR INTERESTS/PRIORITIES] and our work in [GEOGRAPHY/COMMUNITY].

OVERALL SPECIFICATIONS:
- Length: For asks $50K+: 1,100–1,500 words. For asks under $50K: 800–1,000 words (combine sections as noted below).
- Format: [FORMAT PREFERENCE: formal letter / PDF proposal / email proposal – default to formal letter].
- Visual/formatting:
  - Use clear section headings in Title Case (exact titles listed below).
  - Single-spaced, one blank line between paragraphs; bullet lists where specified.
  - For formal letter/PDF: include date and inside address block; place logo on letterhead if available.
  - For email: include a concise subject line and greeting; omit mailing address block.
- Tone: [TONE – default to WARM]. Reading level: ~10th grade. Short paragraphs (2–4 sentences), active voice, specific numbers.
- Personalization: Reference recent donor touchpoints (past 12–18 months) and make explicit links to [DONOR INTERESTS/PRIORITIES].
- If any data is missing, insert a clear placeholder [TK: describe missing item] and generate realistic, good-faith examples (especially for per-unit costs, outcomes, and milestones) based on the project type.

STRUCTURE (use these headings and order)

1. Cover Letter (120–180 words)
- Link [DONOR NAME]’s connection to [MISSION/FOCUS] and our shared values.
- Reference a recent touchpoint (visit, gift, event) from past 12–18 months.
- [OPTIONAL: Weave in ANECDOTE/STORY in 1–2 sentences].
- Preview the invitation to partner (do not state the ask amount yet).

2. Donor Alignment (150–200 words)
- 3–5 bullet points mapping the project to [DONOR INTERESTS/PRIORITIES].
- Use specific touchpoints (date/year and nature of engagement).
- Make explicit connections (e.g., “Your 2023 scholarship gift supporting first-generation students aligns with this project’s focus on …”).

3. Project Overview (250–350 words)
Include:
- What: Concise description of the project/program and scope.
- Why now: Opportunity or timing without alarmist tone.
- Who benefits: Define target population with numbers and geography (e.g., “120 formerly incarcerated adults annually in Maricopa County”).
- How we deliver: Key activities and approach.
- Equity/access considerations: How the project addresses barriers or advances inclusion.
  - Example: “Weekend and evening hours reduce access barriers for caregivers.”
- Risks and mitigations: 1–2 realistic challenges and responses.
  - Example: “Risk: Hiring delays. Mitigation: Shared staffing pool with partners and stipends for practicum students.”

4. Budget & Request (200–250 words)
- Total project cost: [TOTAL PROJECT COST]
- Raised to date: [CURRENT FUNDS RAISED]
- Funding gap: [FUNDING GAP]
Primary ask:
- “We invite you to consider a gift of [PRIMARY ASK AMOUNT] over [YEARS].”
- Show annual pledge schedule (e.g., “$50,000 per year for three years”).
- [If applicable: Note MATCH/CHALLENGE INFO with ratio, cap, and timing.]
Alternative gift levels:
- [ALT ASK 1] would enable [specific scope or component].
- [ALT ASK 2] would enable [expanded scope or duration].
Per-unit cost examples (2–3):
- Example template 1: “$2,500 supports one student for a full academic year (tuition gap, books, tutoring, mentorship).”
- Example template 2: “$15,000 equips one classroom with laptops, connectivity, and teacher training.”
- Use [PER-UNIT COST EXAMPLES] if provided, or generate realistic equivalents based on the project.

5. Outcomes & Measurement (200–250 words)
Present 3–5 outcomes in this format:
- Outcome: [Specific result].
- Baseline: [Current state with number/year].
- Target: [Goal with number and timeframe].
- Data source: [System, survey, or third-party verification].
- Reporting: [REPORTING CADENCE] with format (dashboard + narrative).
Example:
- Outcome: Increase program completion.
- Baseline: 62% (2023 cohort).
- Target: 80% by Year 2.
- Data source: Participant database; external evaluator validates.
- Reporting: Quarterly dashboard; annual narrative summary.

6. Timeline & Milestones (150–200 words)
- Year 1: Month-by-month or quarter-by-quarter milestones.
- Years 2+: Quarter-by-quarter through [YEARS].
- Include 4–6 concrete milestones with dates and brief descriptors.
- Use a simple bulleted timeline.

7. Recognition (100–150 words)
- Present [RECOGNITION OPTIONS] (e.g., named fund, room naming, annual report, website feature).
- Honor [PRIVACY PREFERENCE], including anonymous recognition if desired.
- Frame as “ways we would be honored to recognize your partnership.”

8. Co-Design & Engagement (100–150 words)
Invite input on 2–3 decisions, such as:
- Focus site selection among candidate locations.
- Evaluation emphasis (e.g., employment outcomes vs. skill gains).
- Learning agenda questions to test during pilot.
- [Optional: Mention an advisory role or periodic learning sessions.]
- Emphasize genuine partnership: “Your insights would strengthen this work.”

9. Stewardship & Reporting (100–150 words)
Specify:
- Who reports: role/name (e.g., “Program Director Sarah Chen”).
- Cadence: [REPORTING CADENCE].
- Format: Financial statements plus narrative stories; brief dashboards.
- Engagement: Site or virtual visits, beneficiary story sharing, annual impact briefing.

10. Governance/Approvals (50–75 words)
- [ED/BOARD APPROVAL STATUS] (e.g., “Reviewed by ED and Board Development Committee on [date]”).
- Fiscal controls and restricted fund handling (segregated accounts; audited annually).
- Brief note on financial health/audit status if relevant.

11. Next Steps (75–100 words)
- Invite conversation by [MEETING DATE/DEADLINE] if provided.
- Provide clear contact: [CONTACT INFO].
- Warm close reinforcing partnership.
- Signature block:
  - [SIGNER NAME, TITLE]
  - Phone | Email | Organization website
  - Mailing address
  - For PDF/letter, place logo on letterhead; for email, include a concise subject line and email signature.

QUALITY STANDARDS

Do:
- Use concrete numbers and specific examples throughout.
- Write in active voice and short paragraphs.
- Present partnership and choice while showing confidence in the plan.
- Use “you/your” to stay donor-centered.
- Define any acronyms on first use.
- Include brief transitions between sections to maintain flow.

Avoid:
- Clichés (“transformational,” “game-changer,” “unprecedented”).
- Jargon (“capacity-building,” “value proposition,” “synergies”).
- Desperate urgency (“without your help, this will fail”).
- Vague outcomes (“improve lives”)—specify what will change and by how much.
- Corporate sales language (“deliverables,” “ROI”) unless donor prefers.

For asks under $50K:
- Keep to 800–1,000 words.
- Combine Sections 8–10 into one “Partnership & Next Steps” section (150–200 words) that succinctly covers engagement options, stewardship cadence, and governance/approvals.

Generate the complete proposal now.
```
